Slide 10 · Financing Plan

The plan is largely self-funding — export-credit debt plus released working capital, no equity dilution

How the ~€4.3M is funded, by year (€000)

Export-credit debt (SACE-style) NWC release Operating cash flow

① Export-credit debt

~€1.35M via favorable SACE-style instruments. Moderate ~1.0x leverage gives genuine headroom; precedent of public/development financing (Teramo plant).

② Working-capital release

~€1.9M. Coop/Esselunga carry 60–90+ day terms; reducing that volume shortens the receivables cycle and releases cash from the €75M NWC pile.

③ Equity — contingency only

Reserved as a fallback, not a planned source — preserving family control. Operating cash flow (~€1.09M) covers the balance.

Slide 11 · The Ask & Decision Gates

We ask the Board to fund a bounded ~€791K Year-1 start, with a go/no-go gate every year

What we ask the Board to approve now

  • Ring-fence a Year-1 budget of ~€791K as a separate Board line item
  • Approve the Year-1 hires (US specialist, QA/compliance, logistics coordinator)
  • Authority to initiate Uncle Giuseppe's commercial discussions
  • Commission the MAP feasibility study — the hard gate before Year-2 capital

How each gate minimises risk

Y1 gate: MAP feasibility validates cold-chain economics before any Year-2 commitment. Y3 gate: honest dry-track review (keep ≥15%, else wind down). Y4: Canada reconsidered only on realized US volume. Each year the Board can stop, pivot, or accelerate.

Why the Board should say yes

  • Stay in control: debt + working-capital release, no equity dilution, family ownership intact
  • Low-regret start: Year-1 spend is bounded and mostly recoverable infrastructure
  • Grow the name abroad: premium authenticity rebuilds the brand internationally
  • The window is open now: tariffs settled, CIOO in place, capacity idle
Standing still guarantees decline. This plan offers a financed, gated path back to profit.
